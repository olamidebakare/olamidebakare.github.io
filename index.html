About

I'm a data engineer with a background in healthcare data systems and cloud architecture. I've spent years working with sensitive data in hospital environments — building ETL pipelines, optimising database infrastructure, and learning that every data engineering decision is also a security decision.

My focus is on the intersection of data engineering and cybersecurity. I believe the next generation of cyber resilience will be built not just by security teams, but by data engineers who design security into every pipeline, every warehouse, and every data architecture decision from the ground up.

I hold three degrees including two master's — an MSc in Big Data Technologies from Glasgow Caledonian University and an MIS from the University of Ibadan. I write about data, cybersecurity, and occasionally about what happens when a data engineer tries to make sense of everyday life.
